Of course, I can help with that. It's a very common point of confusion, and you've hit on the most likely culprits. Getting different addresses from the same seed phrase almost always comes down to one thing: the **derivation path**.

Let's break down what's likely happening.

A seed phrase (defined by BIP39) is just the starting point. It's used to generate a master private key. From that single master key, a nearly infinite number of individual private keys (and their corresponding public addresses) can be derived. The **derivation path** is like a set of instructions that tells the wallet which specific key/address to generate from the master key.

If two different wallet tools use two different derivation paths, they will generate two different addresses from the exact same seed phrase.

Here are the most common derivation paths, which also correspond to different Bitcoin address types:

1.  **Legacy (P2PKH):**
    *   **Derivation Path:** `m/44'/0'/0'/0/0`
    *   **Address Format:** Starts with a `1`. (e.g., `1A1zP1eP5QGefi2DMPTfTL5SLmv7DivfNa`)
    *   This is the original address format. Fees are typically higher than with newer formats.

2.  **Nested SegWit (P2SH-P2WPKH):**
    *   **Derivation Path:** `m/49'/0'/0'/0/0`
    *   **Address Format:** Starts with a `3`. (e.g., `3J98t1WpEZ73CNmQviecrnyiWrnqRhWNLy`)
    *   This was an intermediate step to introduce SegWit, offering better compatibility and lower fees than Legacy.

3.  **Native SegWit (Bech32):**
    *   **Derivation Path:** `m/84'/0'/0'/0/0`
    *   **Address Format:** Starts with `bc1q`. (e.g., `bc1qar0srrr7xfkvy5l643lydnw9re59gtzzwf5mdq`)
    *   This is the modern standard. It offers the lowest transaction fees and is the recommended format for most users.

4.  **Taproot (Bech32m):**
    *   **Derivation Path:** `m/86'/0'/0'/0/0`
    *   **Address Format:** Starts with `bc1p`. (e.g., `bc1p5d7rjq7g6rdk2yhzks9smlaqtedr4dekq08ge8ztwac72sfr9rusxg3297`)
