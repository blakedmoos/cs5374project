Think of your seed phrase as the "Master Key" to a building. The **Derivation Path** is the specific map that tells the wallet which "room" (address) to look in for the money. If the wallet looks in the wrong room, it sees an empty room, even though the building (seed) is correct.

In 2017, the Bitcoin ecosystem was transitioning between different address formats, leading to several common derivation paths:

### 1. Legacy (BIP44)
*   **Address Format:** Starts with a **`1`** (e.g., `1A1zP...`)
*   **Derivation Path:** `m/44'/0'/0'`
*   **Context:** This was the standard for most of Bitcoin's early history. If your wallet is from early 2017 or before, this is the most likely path.

### 2. Nested SegWit (BIP49)
*   **Address Format:** Starts with a **`3`** (e.g., `3J98t...`)
*   **Derivation Path:** `m/49'/0'/0'`
*   **Context:** SegWit was activated in August 2017. Many wallets started switching to this format around late 2017 to lower transaction fees.

### 3. Native SegWit (BIP84)
*   **Address Format:** Starts with **`bc1`** (e.g., `bc1q...`)
*   **Derivation Path:** `m/84'/0'/0'`
*   **Context:** This became standard later. It is less likely for a 2017 wallet, but possible if you updated the software later.
