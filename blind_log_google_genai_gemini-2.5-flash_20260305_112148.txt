### How BIP-39 Checksum Validation Works (Conceptually)

1.  **Initial Entropy:** A seed phrase starts with a certain amount of random data, called entropy. For a 12-word phrase, this is typically 128 bits of entropy.
2.  **Checksum Calculation:** The system takes the initial entropy and calculates a cryptographic hash of it (specifically, SHA256). The number of checksum bits depends on the entropy length (e.g., for 128 bits of entropy, 4 bits are used for the checksum).
3.  **Bit Grouping:** The combined entropy + checksum bits are then divided into groups of 11 bits.
4.  **Word Mapping:** Each 11-bit group corresponds to a number between 0 and 2047. This number is used as an index to select a word from the 2048-word BIP-39 word list.
5.  **The Last Word:** Crucially, the last word of the seed phrase is derived from the last 11 bits, which *include* the checksum bits.

*   It converts the words back into their 11-bit binary representations.
