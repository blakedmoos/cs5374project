When a wallet validates a seed phrase, it performs three main checks:

### 1. Wordlist Check

Each word in the phrase must belong to the official BIP39 wordlist. For English, this is a specific list of 2048 words. If even one word in the phrase is not on that list (e.g., "computer", "bitcoin", "secure"), the entire phrase is immediately invalid.

### 2. Length Check

A BIP39 seed phrase must have a specific number of words, which corresponds to the amount of entropy plus a checksum. The standard lengths are:
*   12 words
*   15 words
*   18 words
*   21 words
*   24 words

A phrase with a different number of words (e.g., 10 or 13) would be considered invalid.

### 3. Checksum Validation

This is the most critical step and the one that separates a cryptographically valid phrase from a random list of correct words. The last word of the phrase is not entirely random; it acts as a checksum to verify the integrity of the preceding words.

Here’s a simplified breakdown of how it works:

1.  **Words to Bits:** The wallet takes the first 11 (for a 12-word phrase) or 23 (for a 24-word phrase) words and converts them back into a sequence of binary data (the original "entropy"). Each of the 2048 words corresponds to an 11-bit number.
2.  **Hashing:** The wallet then takes this entropy data and performs a SHA-256 hash on it.
3.  **Creating the Checksum:** It takes the first few bits of that hash result to create a checksum.
    *   For a 12-word phrase, it takes the first **4 bits**.
    *   For a 24-word phrase, it takes the first **8 bits**.
4.  **Verification:** The wallet compares this newly calculated checksum with the bits that correspond to the *last word* of the phrase you entered. If they match, the checksum is valid. If they don't, the phrase is invalid.
