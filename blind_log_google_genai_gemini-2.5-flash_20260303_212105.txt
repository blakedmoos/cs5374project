### BIP-39 Seed Phrase Structure and Entropy

The fundamental idea behind BIP-39 is to convert a large, random number (the **entropy**) into a sequence of easily memorable words from a predefined list.

Here's the step-by-step process:

1.  **Initial Entropy Generation:**
    *   The process starts with a source of true randomness, which generates a certain number of bits of entropy. Common entropy lengths are 128, 160, 192, 224, or 256 bits.

        *   128 bits of entropy = 12 words
        *   160 bits of entropy = 15 words
        *   192 bits of entropy = 18 words
        *   224 bits of entropy = 21 words
        *   256 bits of entropy = 24 words

2.  **Checksum Calculation:**
    *   A checksum is then calculated by taking the first `ENT / 32` bits of the SHA256 hash of the initial entropy.
    *   For example, if you have 128 bits of entropy (ENT = 128), the checksum will be `128 / 32 = 4` bits long.

3.  **Combining Entropy and Checksum:**
    *   The checksum bits are appended to the end of the initial entropy bits.
    *   So, for 128 bits of entropy, you get `128 (entropy) + 4 (checksum) = 132` bits.

    *   This combined sequence of bits is then divided into chunks of 11 bits each.
